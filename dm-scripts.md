# DM Scripts — Copy & Paste Ready

All scripts are short on purpose. Long DMs get ignored.

---

## DM 1 — After Someone Comments on Your Manual Offer Post

Send this within 1 hour of their comment.

---

> Hey [Name]!
>
> Thanks for commenting — happy to write your cover letter.
>
> Just need a few details from you:
>
> 1. Job title you're applying for
> 2. The job description (paste it or share the link)
> 3. Your name, current country, and target country
> 4. Your top 3 skills and years of experience
> 5. Do you need employer sponsorship, or do you already have the right to work?
>
> Send those over and I'll have your letter back to you within 24 hours.

---

## DM 2 — After You Deliver the Manual Letter (Feedback Request)

Send this 24–48 hours after delivering the letter.

---

> Hey [Name], just checking in — did the cover letter work for you?
>
> Even just a quick "yes it was good" or "here's what I'd change" helps a lot.
>
> Also — quick honest question: if there was a tool that did this automatically in 60 seconds for $9/month, would that be worth it to you given how many jobs you're applying for?

---

## DM 3 — Presale Message (If They Said the Letter Was Good)

Send only after they gave positive feedback.

---

> Glad it helped!
>
> I'm actually building a tool that does this automatically — you paste the job description, fill in your details, and get a full letter in under a minute.
>
> I'm taking presale signups this week: $9 now locks in 2 months of access when it launches (normally $9/month after that). Full refund if you don't love it.
>
> Want me to send you the payment link?

---

## DM 4 — Cold Outreach (For People Who Posted Asking for Help)

Use when someone in a Facebook group or Reddit posted asking for cover letter help and you want to reach out without being spammy.

---

> Hey [Name], saw your post about applying for [nursing/IT/visa] jobs in [UK/Canada].
>
> I actually built a free tool specifically for international applicants — it handles the sponsorship part of the letter so it doesn't come across the wrong way to employers.
>
> Want me to send you the link? Takes about 60 seconds to try, no card needed.

---

## DM 5 — Reddit Follow-Up (After Giving Helpful Advice in a Thread)

Use after you've genuinely helped someone in a thread. Do not use this without first providing real value in the comment.

---

> Hey, saw you were asking about cover letters for sponsored roles. Wanted to follow up — I actually built a free tool for exactly this situation.
>
> It's at visaletter.ai — the first 3 letters are free, no card needed.
>
> Happy to help if you have any questions about it.

## Feedback Questions to Ask Every Tester

After delivering a manual letter, ask these 5 questions. You can do it by DM or send a Google Form link.

1. On a scale of 1–5, how good was the cover letter compared to what you would have written yourself?
2. Did it handle the visa/sponsorship part in a way that felt natural and professional?
3. What would you change or improve about it?
4. Would you use this regularly if it was automated and took 60 seconds?
5. What would you expect to pay for unlimited access per month? (Free / $5 / $9 / $15 / More)

Save every answer. This data tells you exactly what to build and how to price it.
